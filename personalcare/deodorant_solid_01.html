Homemade Solid Deodorant

Last Updated: 2020.06.13

Make a solid deodorant that performs similarly to commercial products.

This set of instructions include two active components and three inactive components. The active components are what make the deodorant function either by reducing bacteria or adding a pleasant smell. The inactive components just help as a medium to hold the active components in place on your body.

Active Components

Baking Soda

Baking Soda or Sodium bicarbonate is used as an antimicrobial. [1] It's a common household product and is considered safe. Since it's also a weak base if too much is used it can cause mild skin irritation. Reduce this ingredient as needed.

Essential Oils

Essential oils are extractions from plants, usually fragrant, sometimes with additional properties. There's a wide variety of information on the Internet of mixed quality and so this is just a list of what's worked for me. Different people will have different oils that smell good to them. My suggestion you're unsure where to start is to explore a natural food store, such as whole foods, and smell the different varieties.

I use 10 drops of each oil listed here. Additionally I use two droppers worth of beard oil for additional smells. If you have sensitive skin, I suggest testing small amount of diluted oil on your skin to determine adverse reactions. The science behind each of these oils is linked in the citation section.

  • Tea Tree Oil [2]
  • Clove Oil [3]
  • Bergamont Oil [4]

Inactive Components

Bees Wax

Bees Wax is used as a gelling agent for the deodorant. It has a melting point of about 62°C (144 °F), and at room temperature is solid. It also helps reduce the grittiness of the powdered baking soda and starch. This will also help the deodorant from melting in higher summer temperatures.

Starch

Plant Starch is used as a thickening agent that helps thicken the deodorant's liquid components.

Glycerin

Glycerin is viscous at room temperature and is used to prevent the deodorant from being too stiff. Since it's liquid at room temperature it helps the deodorant flow in solid form.
